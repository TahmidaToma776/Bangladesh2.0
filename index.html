The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

  • We live in a world where the challenges of poverty, inequality, and injustice are ever-present. But I firmly believe that we, as individuals and as a society, have the ability to change the course of history. There is an enormous potential within each of us to make a difference. One of the most powerful tools we have to address these challenges is the concept of social business.Social business is a business that is driven not by profit maximization, but by the pursuit of social goals. It is a business where the primary objective is to solve a social problem—be it in health, education, the environment, or poverty alleviation—while remaining financially sustainable. This is the true potential of human ingenuity, to harness the power of enterprise to serve humanity"

  • Dr. Muhammad Yunus

  • Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh

  • Bangladesh Launches New Satellite

  • Date: 10-01-2025 Time: 10.25 PM

  • Bangladesh recently launched its third satellite, Bangabandhu-3, into orbit. The satellite, which was successfully launched on January 10, 2025, aims to enhance the country's communications capabilities, offering improved internet and telecommunication services.It will also help boost Bangladesh's space exploration and scientific research activities. This launch marks another step forward for the nation’s space ambitions, following the success of its earlier satellites, Bangabandhu-1 and Bangabandhu-2. The satellite is expected to support the development of telemedicine, e-education, disaster management, and various commercial applications.
